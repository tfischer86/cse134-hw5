The Epic Quest to Draw My Cats

Recently, I decided to try using a set of Faber-Castell Pitt Graphite Matt pencils to draw my cat, Hazel.

The Faber-Castell Pitt Graphite Matt pencils are advertised to have less shininess than typical graphite pencils. These pencils have a more subdued finish, which can help create a more natural look.

Since this was my first time using these pencils, I used graph paper since I only have 20 sheets of the good stuff and I didn't want to waste any. Despite this, I think I was able to achieve my goal of a lifelike drawing.

To begin my drawing, I started by sketching the outline of Hazel's face using a mechanical pencil. I usually start drawing the eyes, nose, and mouth, since they're the hardest to get right. In particular, the eyes needed a lot of work before they finally looked real. If they're even slightly off, the whole drawing enters the uncanny valley and just looks creepy.

Next, I filled out the rest of her body. I used a blending stump to give the drawing a soft fluffy look, and I used an eraser to add highlights that make the fur look shiny.

Overall, I spent about 8 hours working on it over a few nights. This was my first time attempting to create a photo-realistic drawing, and I'm pretty happy with how it turned out. I would highly recommend these pencils to anyone who is interested in drawing.
