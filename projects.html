PlatformerNEAT

I implemented the NEAT algorithm and evolved neural networks to play a simple platformer game.

NEAT (NeuroEvolution of Augmenting Topologies) is a genetic algorithm developed for evolving artificial neural networks by adding, removing and reorganizing neurons and connections, in order to find the best network structure and weights for a given task. The algorithm was created by Kenneth O. Stanley and Risto Miikkulainen in 2002.

The NEAT algorithm begins by generating a population of ANNs with small, random structures. Each ANN in the population is evaluated on a given task, such as playing a game or recognizing objects in images. The algorithm then selects the best-performing ANNs from the population, and uses them as parents to create a new generation of ANNs with slightly modified structures.

It's extremely satisfying to see the networks complete a level, but it can also be frustrating to watch!
